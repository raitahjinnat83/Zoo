Elephant

As the largest land mammal, elephants use their trunks for many purposes, such as sucking up water to hydrate. They also have tusks they use for self-defense and to gather food. However, the illegal ivory trade targets elephants for their tusks, which is why their conservation status is volatile and endangered. Elephants are herbivores which mainly eat grass and fruit. They are highly intelligent and usually peaceful animals, found across diverse habitats such as the forest and desert across countries in both the continents Africa and Asia. Asian elephants have a shorter lifespan of around 50 years than African elephants, which can live up to 60 to 70 years.
